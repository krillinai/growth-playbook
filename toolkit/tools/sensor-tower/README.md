# Sensor Tower

> A digital market-intelligence platform covering mobile apps, usage, audiences, advertising, web, gaming, and related competitive trends. Use it to benchmark markets and form hypotheses, then validate decisions with first-party store, product, advertising, and financial data.

## Where It Fits

```text
Market and category research
-> competitor and audience baseline
-> acquisition, retention, or monetization hypothesis
-> product and campaign decision
-> first-party validation
```

Sensor Tower is useful when internal data explains only your own product and you need a comparable external view of categories, competitors, countries, publishers, ads, or audience behavior. It cannot reveal a competitor's complete internal economics or prove why a market changed.

## Core Jobs

### 1. Market and Category Sizing

- Compare estimated downloads, revenue, active use, engagement, and category movement across markets.
- Identify whether a change appears company-specific, category-wide, seasonal, geographic, or platform-related.
- Use multiple windows because launches, promotions, holidays, and reporting changes can distort short periods.

### 2. Competitor App Performance

- Benchmark estimated downloads, revenue, rankings, ratings, reviews, keywords, and publisher portfolios.
- Compare growth shape, market mix, release timing, and category concentration rather than copying one feature or campaign.
- Separate observed public events from inferred causes.

### 3. Usage, Engagement, and Retention Research

- Compare available estimates for active users, sessions, time spent, retained users, churned users, and usage intensity.
- Use the data to select competitors, cohorts, countries, or behaviors for deeper investigation.
- Validate your own retention and value events with product analytics; external engagement does not explain customer value by itself.

### 4. Advertising and Creative Intelligence

- Study advertiser, publisher, network, share-of-voice, creative, and campaign patterns covered by the platform.
- Identify sustained messages, formats, markets, and apparent campaign changes that may deserve testing.
- Do not infer spend efficiency, incrementality, conversion, or payback from observed ad presence alone.

### 5. Audience and Cross-Platform Research

- Compare audience engagement, app interaction, ad exposure, and supported web-to-app patterns.
- Look for overlap, adjacent demand, media behavior, and possible cross-channel journeys.
- Treat audience panels and modeled overlap as directional evidence whose coverage varies by market and segment.

### 6. Internationalization and Market Selection

- Compare country-level category size, competitor strength, usage, monetization, rankings, and advertising activity.
- Use the comparison to prioritize localization research, not to assume that product value and go-to-market transfer automatically.
- Add language, payments, regulation, devices, distribution, culture, and local competitor evidence before entering a market.

## Measurement Map

| Tool signal | Useful question | Required validation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Download estimate | Is acquisition or category demand changing? | Store-console installs, source quality, activation, incrementality |
| Revenue estimate | Which products or markets may monetize strongly? | First-party revenue, refunds, fees, margin, payer cohorts |
| Active-user or session estimate | Which apps appear to sustain usage? | Product value events, cohort retention, frequency |
| Retained or churned-user estimate | Where may engagement quality differ? | Internal cohort definitions and natural usage cycle |
| Ranking and keyword signal | How visible is an app in store discovery? | Store impressions, page conversion, qualified installs |
| Advertising SOV or creative | Which messages and markets receive sustained investment? | Platform spend, experiments, CAC, payback, retained quality |
| Audience overlap | Which adjacent audiences may be relevant? | Customer research, CRM, product and campaign tests |

## Practical Workflow

1. Define the market question, decision, category, countries, platforms, competitors, and time window.
2. Record metric definitions and avoid comparing incompatible products, markets, or maturity stages.
3. Establish category and competitor baselines across acquisition, usage, monetization, and advertising.
4. Mark observations separately from causal hypotheses.
5. Prioritize a product, localization, channel, creative, or market experiment.
6. Validate with app-store consoles, product analytics, advertising platforms, CRM, revenue, and qualitative research.
7. Revisit external estimates when methodology, coverage, category definitions, or platform policies change.

## Limits

- Downloads, revenue, active use, retention, advertising, audience, and competitor values may be estimated or modeled rather than audited company data.
- Different products can define active users, revenue, churn, markets, and categories differently.
- Correlation between advertising, rankings, downloads, usage, and revenue does not establish causality.
- Official pages describe data sources including consumer panels and app-store APIs; coverage and methodology should be checked for the relevant product and market.
- Features, product names, database access, pricing, and terms can change.
- Follow platform terms, privacy requirements, competition law, and applicable regulation.

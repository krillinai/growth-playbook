# Referral & Expansion

## Case 1: Dropbox - Value-Aligned Referral and Collaborative Expansion

**Context.** Early Dropbox value came from file synchronization and storage. A referral reward could therefore reinforce an already understood product benefit rather than introduce an unrelated prize.

**Mechanism.** Additional storage benefited both participation and continued product use. Referred users entered a product whose utility could later support file sharing, collaboration, team adoption, and product-led commercial expansion.

```text
Must-have storage value -> referral with storage benefit -> recipient activation
-> retained storage or sharing -> collaboration -> team or account expansion
```

**Evidence to retain.** The company page connects referral participation to recipient activation, retained quality, reward cost, and abuse. Later product-led evidence treats shared files and collaborative use as distribution and expansion signals without assuming they prove buying readiness.

**Transfer boundary.** The reward cannot create product-market fit. Storage economics, service cost, fraud, identity, inactive seats, authority, and account value must be measured. The case does not establish one continuous causal history across Dropbox periods.

## Case 2: Facebook / Meta - Social Graph and Network Expansion

**Context.** Early Facebook value depended on relevant people being present in a dense identity-based network. Expansion moved from campuses to high schools, broader public access, and international markets.

**Mechanism.** Friend discovery and onboarding helped new users reach social value. Existing participation, invitations, and word of mouth could expose more people, while added relevant connections could increase value inside a local social graph.

```text
Relevant local network -> friend connection -> retained social value
-> word of mouth or invitation -> new participant -> denser network
-> adjacent community or market expansion
```

**Evidence to retain.** Participant accounts emphasize retention, activation proxies, friend discovery, growth accounting, instrumentation, experiments, and internationalization. The reusable method is to connect propagation to retained network value rather than copy a memorable activation threshold.

**Transfer boundary.** Facebook's campus sequence, identity graph, scale, data, market timing, and social context are unusual. Invitations, total connections, or monthly activity do not independently establish participant welfare or causal network value.

## Case 3: Uber - Local Liquidity and Geographic Expansion

**Context.** Riders and drivers had to be available in the same geography and time window. Aggregate marketplace size could not compensate for weak local liquidity.

**Mechanism.** More relevant driver availability could reduce pickup time and improve fulfillment, which could increase rider value and repeat demand. Better demand and utilization could strengthen supply economics. A repeatable local unit then created a basis for city expansion.

```text
Local supply -> reliable match and trip -> retained rider demand
-> utilization and supply value -> denser local network
-> repeatable operating unit -> adjacent city expansion
```

**Evidence to retain.** The company page documents atomic networks, hard-side diagnosis, local supply operations, successful trips, cross-side retention, participant economics, incentives, and city-level testing. Each arrow remains a market-specific hypothesis.

**Transfer boundary.** Capital, labor pools, regulation, safety, multi-homing, frequency, geography, incentives, and local operations shape the mechanism. Copying a rideshare interface does not reproduce liquidity or supplier economics.

## Case 4: Airtable - Collaboration as Distribution and Account Value

**Context.** Airtable's flexibility allows many workflows but creates a blank-state problem. A user must build something useful before inviting others into a meaningful collaborative context.

**Mechanism.** Customer insight and templates make a workflow concrete; segmented onboarding helps a user reach first value; a shared artifact or workflow then brings collaborators into a workspace and can deepen team adoption.

```text
Concrete use case -> first useful workflow -> contextual invitation
-> collaborator value -> repeated multi-user use -> retained account breadth
```

**Evidence to retain.** Lauryn Isford discusses a week-four multi-user collaboration measure as a stronger downstream signal than isolated interface completion. The company case connects early actions with later collaboration and retention rather than treating invitations as success.

**Transfer boundary.** Collaboration is not the right value signal for every product or segment. Roles, permissions, workspace readiness, account ownership, active-seat quality, and coordination cost determine whether more users deepen value.

## Case 5: ByteDance - Rewarded Referral and Portfolio Expansion

**Context.** Consumer products used cash, coins, coupons, referral rewards, Lite products, seasonal campaigns, and product cross-promotion for different acquisition, retention, and expansion objectives.

**Mechanism.** Referral can be decomposed into sender participation, recipients, conversion, retained quality, cost, and abuse. Shared incentive infrastructure can coordinate eligibility, accounting, anti-fraud, experimentation, and cross-product distribution.

```text
Product value -> eligible behavior -> referral or portfolio incentive
-> recipient or adjacent-product activation -> post-reward retention
-> portfolio value -> fraud and cannibalization decision
```

**Evidence to retain.** The case distinguishes incentive types, treats K-factor as incomplete without retention, embeds risk control, checks behavior after rewards decline, and evaluates Lite or portfolio growth against audience overlap and total value.

**Transfer boundary.** Reported figures and internal outcomes are not independently verified. Smaller companies should not copy subsidy systems without identity, device, payment, graph, accounting, experimentation, and incident-response capability.

## Cross-Company Patterns

### Value Must Precede Propagation

Dropbox requires useful storage, Facebook relevant connections, Uber a successful trip, Airtable a useful workflow, and ByteDance a product worth amplifying. Prompts, rewards, and cross-promotion cannot manufacture durable value.

### The Recipient or New Scope Needs First Value

The loop is incomplete at send, open, signup, seat purchase, or product attach. The recipient, collaborator, adjacent product user, or new city must receive a product-specific value event and retain it.

### Expansion Uses Different Entities

Facebook and Uber expand network units, Airtable expands workspace participation, Dropbox can expand user, team, or product adoption, and ByteDance can expand across products and segments. One expansion rate cannot compare these entities without a common definition.

### Incrementality and Trust Constrain Scale

Dropbox rewards and ByteDance incentives can replace organic behavior or attract abuse. Facebook invitations and Airtable collaboration can create fatigue or permission risk. Uber subsidies can hide weak local economics. Gross propagation must be reconciled with causal lift, participant value, and full cost.

## Transfer Matrix

| Product condition | Most relevant case | What to borrow | What not to assume |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A product-aligned benefit can reward both sides | Dropbox | Trigger after value, align reward, validate recipient retention and cost | That a double-sided reward creates PMF or healthy account expansion |
| Relevant participation improves a social experience | Facebook / Meta | Connect onboarding, graph formation, retention, and adjacent network sequencing | That invitation volume or a universal connection threshold proves value |
| Value depends on local cross-side liquidity | Uber | Define atomic network, hard side, transaction quality, and city expansion criteria | That aggregate scale or subsidies create sustainable local economics |
| Shared artifacts create multi-user workflows | Airtable | Carry context into invitations and measure repeated collaboration | That more seats or invites automatically deepen account value |
| Multiple products share incentives and distribution | ByteDance | Shared controls, post-reward cohorts, portfolio incrementality, anti-abuse | That campaign spikes or one product's growth increase total portfolio value |

## Evidence and Limits

- Company evidence comes from participant interviews, public talks, articles, and external analyses rather than complete internal causal histories.
- The cases span different periods, entities, products, markets, and business models; their propagation or expansion metrics are not directly comparable.
- Exact holdouts, incremental lift, retention, contribution, fraud, and cannibalization data are incomplete.
- Company outcomes also reflect product value, market structure, capital, timing, operations, regulation, many teams, and mechanisms not documented here.
- Inclusion documents operating patterns and does not endorse spam, coercive invitations, unauthorized access, unsafe labor practices, manipulative rewards, or any individual company decision.

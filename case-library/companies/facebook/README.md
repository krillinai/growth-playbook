# Facebook / Meta

> Facebook's growth history spans campus expansion, activation, retention, experimentation, internationalization, and later multi-product operations at Meta.

**[English](README.md)｜[简体中文](README.zh.md)**

## Company Overview

Facebook is one of the most influential examples of product-led consumer growth. Its early teams connected social value, activation behavior, retention cohorts, instrumentation, experimentation, internationalization, and organization-wide metrics into a repeatable operating system.

## Growth Stages

| Stage | Growth question | Representative work | Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Campus network | How can a dense, identity-based network establish initial value? | College-by-college rollout and real-identity social graph | Reference context |
| Broader access | How can the product expand beyond its original community? | High schools, public access, onboarding, and friend discovery | Covered in early-growth case |
| Scaled growth system | How can the company diagnose and compound retention? | Growth accounting, cohort analysis, instrumentation, and experiments | Covered in early-growth case |
| International expansion | How can language and access barriers be removed? | Community translation and market prioritization | Covered in early-growth case |
| Multi-product Meta era | How do growth, data, marketing, and research operate across products? | Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, and company-wide systems | Future case direction |

# Grubhub

> A marketplace case centered on restaurant supply, diner demand, delivery-network evolution, local density, and the fact that network effects can be displaced by a competing model.

**[English](README.md)｜[简体中文](README.zh.md)**

## Marketplace Context

Grubhub connected diners with restaurants. Marketplace value depended on relevant restaurant selection, local demand, ordering reliability, and later the operational capability to support delivery.

Casey Winters' accounts connect early Grubhub experience with later marketplace analysis. They are participant perspectives, not a complete company history.

## Supply and Demand

Restaurant inventory was part of the product. A large national restaurant count could not guarantee that a diner in a specific location found relevant, available options.

```text
Relevant local restaurants
-> useful selection for diners
-> more completed orders
-> stronger restaurant value
-> denser local marketplace
```

Each transition requires measurement. Selection without availability, order quality, repeat demand, or workable restaurant economics does not close the loop.

## Delivery Changed the Network

Winters discusses adding a driver application at Grubhub and later competition from DoorDash and Uber Eats. Delivery introduced another side and another operational system: drivers, dispatch, timing, fulfillment, and service quality.

Competitors could build a different network effect by controlling delivery supply and improving the end-to-end transaction. A marketplace strong in restaurant aggregation could therefore lose advantage when the basis of competition shifted toward logistics and fulfillment.

## Network Effects Are Conditional

Useful moat measures include:

| Layer | Example signal |
| --- | --- |
| Supply | Relevant restaurant coverage and exclusivity |
| Demand | Repeat diners and local order density |
| Liquidity | Successful orders within an acceptable time |
| Fulfillment | Delivery availability, accuracy, and reliability |
| Economics | Restaurant, driver, platform, and diner economics |
| Defense | Multi-homing, switching, unique supply, and service differentiation |

Historical scale is not sufficient. If supply multi-homes, demand follows better fulfillment, or a competitor changes the value proposition, the network can weaken.

## Category and Geographic Expansion

Expansion should reuse real strengths rather than only add nominal inventory. New markets and categories need compatible demand, supply, operations, frequency, and economics.

Winters' retrospective view that Grubhub should have acted differently is not a controlled counterfactual. It is useful for identifying strategic questions, not proving one alternate plan would have won.

## Evidence and Limits

- Public retrospectives compress years of product, operational, competitive, and organizational change.
- DoorDash and Uber Eats comparisons do not isolate every factor behind market outcomes.
- Grubhub results reflect restaurants, diners, drivers, teams, capital, regulation, geography, and timing.
- Strategic alternatives are hypotheses, not observed counterfactual outcomes.

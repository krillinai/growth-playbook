# Eventbrite

> A limited product-organization case centered on sustainable acquisition, CPO leadership, growth ownership, and the challenge of matching product structure to changing market conditions.

**[English](README.md)｜[简体中文](README.zh.md)**

## Context

Casey Winters served as Eventbrite's Chief Product Officer and managed product, design, research, and growth marketing. His public discussions use that experience to examine product leadership, growth strategy, organizational communication, and the limits of standard frameworks.

The available evidence is stronger on operating judgment than on specific Eventbrite experiments or causal company outcomes.

## Sustainable Acquisition

Winters distinguishes early “kindle” work from scalable “fire” strategies. Manual acquisition can help identify a retained segment, while scalable channels should be added only after the product, customer, and mechanism are sufficiently understood.

For an event marketplace, supply and demand can vary by location, category, date, organizer quality, and event frequency. Aggregate traffic or event count cannot establish retained value for organizers and attendees.

## Product and Growth Leadership

The CPO material emphasizes:

- communicating strategy and tradeoffs before decision meetings;
- making unglamorous product improvements legible to executives;
- defining what product, growth, design, research, and marketing own;
- protecting existing value while deciding where continued scaling is justified;
- avoiding frameworks that obscure the actual constraint.

These are organization principles rather than proof that one structure produced Eventbrite's results.

## Growth Boundaries

Growth work needs authority over relevant levers, but broad ownership creates maintenance and coordination cost. Product leadership should define outcomes, decision rights, interfaces between functions, and when a temporary growth problem becomes stable product ownership.

Market shocks and structural category changes also limit optimization. Better execution cannot always compensate for a changed event environment or business model constraint.

## Evidence and Limits

- Public interviews provide limited Eventbrite-specific metrics, experiments, cohorts, and economics.
- General product-leadership advice should not be treated as a causal account of company performance.
- Eventbrite outcomes reflect organizers, attendees, category dynamics, market shocks, many teams, and company decisions.

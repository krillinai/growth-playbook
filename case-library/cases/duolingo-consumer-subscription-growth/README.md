## Case Summary

Duolingo's subscription system sits on top of a freemium learning product. Broad access and international reach create acquisition; product habit and recognizable communication encourage return; subscription work converts part of that use into revenue.

The system cannot be reduced to a mascot, streak, paywall, or viral social account. The sources suggest a portfolio of product, brand, market, and monetization mechanisms that need separate measurement.

```text
Accessible product value
-> Organic and international reach
-> Habit and brand recognition
-> Subscription conversion
-> Retention and resurrection
-> Experimentation and reinvestment
```

## 1. Decompose Subscription Growth

Gotthilf frames B2C subscription growth through multiple areas rather than one conversion rate. A practical model separates:

```text
Qualified acquisition
x Activation
x Paid conversion
x Subscription retention
x Revenue per retained subscriber
```

This prevents a paywall win from hiding weaker learning engagement, lower-quality acquisition, or later cancellation.

## 2. Use Organic Distribution Without Confusing Reach and Value

Duolingo's product copy, reminders, and social participation give people something recognizable to discuss. Organic attention can reduce dependence on paid reach, but attention is an acquisition input rather than proof of learning or subscription health.

Teams should connect channel exposure to qualified visits, activation, retained learning behavior, conversion, and brand guardrails.

## 3. Expand Markets Beyond Literal Translation

Internationalization increases the addressable audience, but requires more than translating interface strings. Market demand, local channels, cultural interpretation, payment conditions, device constraints, and operating focus affect adoption.

Expansion should be evaluated market by market. Aggregate global growth can hide weak retention or monetization in individual regions.

## 4. Balance Exploration and Optimization

Cheng's explore-exploit model gives mature growth teams two portfolios:

- **Exploit:** improve known onboarding, paywall, pricing, messaging, and retention surfaces.
- **Explore:** test new loops, products, audiences, monetization surfaces, or ways to create habit.

Without exploration, experiment systems can become highly efficient at producing small local gains. Without exploitation, promising discoveries may never become reliable systems.

## 5. Measure Retention and Resurrection Separately

Continuous retained use and returning after inactivity are different behaviors. Both matter, but combining them can make a churn problem look healthier.

A useful view includes new, retained, resurrected, and churned users, segmented by free and paid state, market, acquisition source, and learning intent.

## 6. Make Gamification Serve Learning

Cheng separates gamification into the core loop, a longer-term metagame, and a user's profile or identity. These systems can make progress visible and support habit.

The guardrail is learning value. If users optimize points while avoiding meaningful practice, engagement rises without fulfilling the product's job.

## Transferable Method

1. Decompose subscription growth into acquisition, activation, conversion, retention, and revenue quality.
2. Segment each measure by market, channel, product state, and user intent.
3. Treat brand and social reach as hypotheses about qualified acquisition.
4. Maintain separate explore and exploit portfolios with explicit resource allocation.
5. Report retained, resurrected, and churned users separately.
6. Map each gamification mechanic to a real value-producing behavior.
7. Use guardrails for learning quality, trust, notification fatigue, and cancellation.

## What Not to Copy Directly

- Do not imitate Duolingo's tone without equivalent brand permission and risk controls.
- Do not add points, streaks, or notifications without a value-based core loop.
- Do not treat social reach as evidence of subscription conversion or retention.
- Do not apply consumer subscription benchmarks across different categories and billing models without adjustment.

## Evidence and Limits

- The case combines participant accounts from different periods and functions; the sequence is an editorial synthesis.
- Complete internal experiments, cohort definitions, attribution, and subscription economics are not available in these sources.
- Product quality, learning efficacy, pricing, market timing, team execution, and brand all contribute to outcomes.
- Public company growth or revenue figures are not used here as causal proof for individual methods.

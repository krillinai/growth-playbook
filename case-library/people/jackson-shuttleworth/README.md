## Growth-Related Work

Shuttleworth leads product work on Duolingo's retention team, including the streak system that records consecutive days of learning. His account shows how a simple counter evolved into a broader system of goals, feedback, recovery, celebrations, and reminders.

The value of this work is not the instruction to add a streak to every product. It is a detailed example of aligning a retention mechanic with a recurring user goal, then improving it through many experiments without allowing the mechanic to replace the underlying learning activity.

## Core Growth Ideas

### 1. A Streak Must Reinforce the Product's Core Value

A streak is useful when the repeated action is already valuable to the user. In Duolingo's case, the mechanic makes daily learning progress visible. If the counted behavior is trivial or unrelated to the user's goal, the counter can increase activity without improving retention quality.

### 2. Clarity Comes Before Elaborate Motivation

Users need to understand what keeps a streak alive, when the day ends, and what state they are in. Clear status, consistent rules, and immediate feedback reduce accidental loss before the team adds more complex rewards or celebrations.

### 3. Recovery Can Protect Long-Term Habit

Streak Freeze reduces the cost of an occasional missed day. It preserves accumulated commitment while allowing real life to interrupt the routine. Recovery mechanics need limits so they support return rather than make the daily behavior meaningless.

### 4. Test the System, Not Only the Counter

Duolingo's streak work spans entry points, visual states, milestones, notifications, recovery, celebrations, and paid features. Shuttleworth discusses learning from more than 600 streak-related experiments. The reported volume shows iteration depth, not proof that test count itself creates growth.

### 5. Notifications Are Part of the Habit Loop

Reminders work best when timing, context, copy, and user state are connected. More notifications can raise short-term returns while increasing fatigue or opt-outs, so engagement gains require guardrails for trust and notification health.

### 6. Monetization Must Not Destroy the Retention Mechanic

Recovery and convenience can support paid value, but charging at a moment of loss can feel coercive. Monetization tests should protect comprehension, fairness, learning behavior, and long-term retention rather than optimize immediate payment alone.

### 7. High Velocity Requires a Clear Product Review System

Experiment volume depends on focused ownership, reusable infrastructure, explicit hypotheses, rapid review, and captured learning. Teams still need judgment about which surfaces deserve repeated optimization and which require larger product changes.

## Streak System Summary

```text
Valuable recurring behavior
-> Clear daily goal and status
-> Immediate progress feedback
-> Contextual reminder
-> Milestone and identity
-> Limited recovery after interruption
-> Return to the core behavior
```

## Evidence and Limits

- Product history and experiment volume are presented through Shuttleworth's participant account.
- The interview does not provide complete experiment designs, effect sizes, confidence intervals, cohort tables, or notification opt-out data.
- Duolingo's learning frequency, brand, audience, product scale, and mature experiment infrastructure limit direct transfer.
- Streaks may correlate with retention because committed users adopt them; causal contribution must be evaluated through controlled tests and cohorts.

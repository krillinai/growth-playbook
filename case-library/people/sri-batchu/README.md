## Core Growth Ideas

### 1. Separate Customer Acquisition and Expansion

New-customer growth and existing-customer expansion have different constraints, owners, and metrics. Combining them can hide weak acquisition or weak product depth.

### 2. Pair Volume With Efficiency

Pipeline, customers, or revenue volume should be evaluated alongside payback and contribution margin. Batchu prefers payback periods for understanding when invested cash returns, while recognizing that assumptions and cohort maturity matter.

### 3. Use More Than One Value Metric When Needed

A single North Star can oversimplify multi-sided or multi-product businesses. A small set of value and economic measures may represent the system better, provided priorities remain clear.

### 4. Fail Conclusively

Negative experiments are useful when they resolve an important hypothesis. An inconclusive test wastes time because it changes no decision. Good design, instrumentation, sample size, and precommitted criteria improve learning quality.

### 5. Structure Follows the Constraint

Growth-team org charts are less important than clear outcomes, access to product and engineering, decision speed, and cross-functional accountability.

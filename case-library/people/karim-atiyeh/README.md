## Core Growth Ideas

### 1. Give B2B Users Consumer-Grade Experience

Business software users still value clarity, speed, and low friction. Better experience can differentiate a product, but must coexist with controls, auditability, security, and complex workflows.

### 2. Compete With Day-One Urgency

Incumbent scale does not remove startup opportunity. A team can win through focus, faster feedback, and willingness to rebuild assumptions, provided urgency does not degrade quality or trust.

### 3. Make the Value Proposition Economically Distinct

Ramp shifted from payments toward a broader finance platform. Differentiation depends on helping customers control and automate spend, not only issuing another corporate card.

### 4. Hire for Spikiness

Atiyeh values candidates with unusually strong capabilities rather than uniformly average profiles. Teams must combine complementary strengths and still require collaboration, judgment, and role fit.

### 5. Technical Builders Can Cross Functional Boundaries

AI and better tooling let technical people prototype in product, marketing, and operations. Direct building shortens feedback loops; domain knowledge and customer understanding remain necessary.

## Growth-Related Work

Egner describes Airtable's early marketing as an effort to understand highly engaged users and turn their language, use cases, and feedback into product and distribution decisions. Customer success and marketing operated closely because conversations with users supplied both product insight and credible stories.

Her account is useful for products that serve many use cases. It shows why a flexible product cannot rely only on broad category messaging: teams must identify who gets unusual value, what they build, and how that proof helps the next user understand the product.

## Core Growth Ideas

### 1. Start With Super-Users

Highly engaged users reveal concrete jobs, vocabulary, objections, and unexpected workflows. Studying them can improve positioning, onboarding, templates, and the roadmap at the same time.

### 2. Connect Customer Success and Marketing

Customer success hears the user's problem in context; marketing turns recurring patterns into clearer education and distribution. A short feedback path reduces the risk that campaigns drift away from product reality.

### 3. Use Brand Selectively

Egner discusses Airtable's use of billboards as a way for a small company to appear credible and present in a competitive market. The reusable principle is channel-message fit and concentration, not that startups should buy outdoor advertising.

### 4. Treat Templates as Product Education

Templates make an adaptable product legible by showing a starting state and a recognizable job. They can improve discovery and activation, but their contribution to revenue depends on audience intent and downstream behavior.

### 5. Avoid Activity Without a Growth Thesis

PR, campaigns, and content should support a defined audience, product behavior, or strategic goal. Visibility alone does not establish activation, retention, or commercial impact.

## Operating Summary

```text
Find highly engaged users
-> Capture jobs, language, and friction
-> Feed insight into product and positioning
-> Turn successful workflows into stories and templates
-> Measure adoption and retained use
```

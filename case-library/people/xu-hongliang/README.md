## Growth-Related Work

Xu describes more than a decade of user-growth work across the evolution from acquisition, ASO, and referral mechanics toward platform infrastructure, automated delivery, creative industrialization, and risk control. At ByteDance, he worked on international growth and discusses TikTok, Douyin, Toutiao, Lite products, and shared growth services.

His interviews are participant recollections recorded in 2026. They provide operational detail and hypotheses, but are not official ByteDance documentation or an independent causal history.

## Core Growth Ideas

### 1. Growth Can Become Shared Infrastructure

Instead of treating user growth as campaign support, ByteDance organized reusable capabilities around forecasting, attribution, delivery, creative assets, device change, re-engagement, push, incentives, and cross-product traffic.

### 2. Optimize Over a Long Enough Value Horizon

Short payback windows can reject users or markets that become valuable later. Xu discusses predictive LTV and multiple ROI horizons. Forecasting extends decision time, but model error and changing cohorts require calibration and guardrails.

### 3. Automate Repeated Decisions, Preserve Judgment

Delivery systems can encode the work of media buyers and operate at greater scale. Humans remain responsible for objectives, data quality, creative direction, exceptional events, audit, and risk.

### 4. Creative Production and Risk Control Must Scale Together

Higher asset volume increases learning but also policy, brand, fraud, and social risk. Tagging, review layers, permissions, and incident feedback are part of the growth system rather than external compliance.

### 5. Incentives Need Segmentation and Anti-Abuse

Cash, coins, coupons, referrals, and Lite products can change acquisition and retention behavior. They can also attract low-quality users, distort recommendations, cannibalize a main product, and create fraud. Audience isolation and risk control are core design requirements.

### 6. Localize the Growth System, Not Only the Product

International growth requires local market priorities, channels, creative context, regulation, device ecosystems, and teams. Domestic tactics and benchmarks do not transfer unchanged.

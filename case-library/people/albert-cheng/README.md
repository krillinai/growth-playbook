## Growth-Related Work

Cheng compares growth systems across three large consumer products rather than presenting one universal playbook. His framework separates exploitation of known opportunities from exploration for new surfaces, audiences, and product mechanics.

This distinction is useful when mature experimentation programs become efficient at optimizing existing funnels but weak at finding the next growth curve.

## Core Growth Ideas

### 1. Balance Explore and Exploit

Exploit work improves known funnels through accumulated evidence. Explore work tests larger uncertainties, such as a new monetization surface or product loop. A portfolio needs both because local optimization eventually reaches diminishing returns.

### 2. Choose Freemium or Trial From Product Behavior

The decision depends on how quickly value appears, how habit forms, marginal cost, and whether free usage creates distribution. Neither model is universally superior.

### 3. Separate Retention From Resurrection

Returning inactive users can be a meaningful source of growth, but resurrected users should be measured separately from continuously retained cohorts. Combining them can hide churn and distort product health.

### 4. Scale Experimentation Without Narrowing Strategy

High experiment volume requires shared infrastructure, clear decision rules, and a culture that accepts negative results. It must still reserve capacity for bets that cannot be evaluated through a small interface test.

### 5. Build Gamification Around the Core Loop

Gamification works when progress systems reinforce the reason people use the product. Cheng separates the core loop, longer-term metagame, and identity or profile. Points and streaks detached from real value can produce activity without durable retention.

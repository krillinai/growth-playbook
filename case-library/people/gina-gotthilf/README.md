## Growth-Related Work

Gotthilf describes Duolingo's consumer growth as a combination of product value, organic distribution, a distinctive brand voice, internationalization, and subscription improvement. Her account connects creative marketing with product and business-model work rather than treating social reach as the entire growth engine.

She also emphasizes learning from failed attempts. A campaign or localized experience is useful when it creates evidence about user motivation, not only when its headline metric rises.

## Core Growth Ideas

### 1. Subscription Growth Has Multiple Levers

Consumer subscription growth cannot be reduced to a paywall test. Acquisition volume, conversion, retention, pricing, and market coverage all affect the result and must be diagnosed separately.

### 2. Organic Growth Depends on Remarkability

People share products and brands that give them something worth discussing. Distinctive product moments, recognizable copy, and culturally relevant communication can create organic distribution when they remain connected to user value.

### 3. Preserve a Coherent Brand Voice

Duolingo's playful, sometimes passive-aggressive reminders became recognizable because the language was consistent with the product experience. A strong voice requires boundaries; random provocation is not a strategy.

### 4. Social Channels Need Native Participation

Duolingo's TikTok presence is an example of adapting to a platform's formats and culture. Reach still needs to be evaluated alongside product interest, activation, retention, and brand risk.

### 5. Internationalization Is More Than Translation

Market expansion involves local demand, language, channels, payments, device conditions, culture, and operating priorities. Literal translation does not ensure that positioning or onboarding works in a new market.

### 6. Expand Responsibility From Demonstrated Results

Gotthilf says she joined Duolingo to work on Brazil, then took on Argentina, Mexico, Europe, and eventually growth leadership as the market work produced results. The broader principle is to widen scope after a team demonstrates learning and impact in a bounded area.

### 7. Investigate Strong Negative Results

With limited engineering support, her early growth work revisited notifications even though others believed the surface had already been optimized. She argues that a statistically meaningful negative result can identify a user moment with high sensitivity. Revert the harmful change, then investigate why that moment matters rather than treating the experiment as useless.

## Evidence and Limits

- Company practices are presented through Gotthilf's participant account.
- The short 20VC video compresses a longer career and experiment history; it does not provide complete role dates, experiment designs, or underlying metrics.
- Organic reach and subscription growth do not by themselves prove which individual tactic caused an outcome.
- Duolingo's brand permission, category, product habit, and scale make direct imitation risky.

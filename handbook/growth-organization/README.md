# Growth Organization

> Growth organization assigns ownership, decision rights, capabilities, and learning around the current growth constraint. There is no universal growth org chart.

**[English](README.md)｜[简体中文](README.zh.md)**

## Organization Model

```text
Growth constraint -> accountable outcome -> cross-functional team
                  -> decision rights -> shared capabilities
                  -> review and learning -> next constraint
```

Common shapes include a centralized team, embedded teams, a functional center of excellence, and a hybrid platform-plus-local model. Choose based on product maturity, constraint, dependencies, and shared capabilities.

## Operating Dimensions

| Dimension | Question | Signal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Outcome ownership | Who owns a customer-value result? | Clear owner and decision |
| Decision rights | What can the team change without escalation? | Dependency wait and reversals |
| Capability access | Can product, data, engineering, design, and distribution act together? | Cycle time and blocked work |
| Learning system | Are evidence and failures reused? | Follow-through and repeated mistakes |
| Sustainability | Does pace preserve quality and people? | Incidents, rework, attrition |

## Diagnosis

| Pattern | Check |
| --- | --- |
| Many ideas, slow delivery | Dependencies, product access, decision rights |
| Fast experiments, little business movement | Weak constraint selection or fragmented ownership |
| Central team becomes a queue | Over-centralization and unclear self-service boundaries |
| Embedded teams duplicate systems | Missing shared platform or standards |
| Growth conflicts with product quality | Misaligned outcomes and absent guardrails |

## Operating Method

1. Name the current constraint and accountable customer-value outcome.
2. Map required functions, dependencies, and shared capabilities.
3. Assign one owner and explicit reversible and irreversible decision rights.
4. Set outcome, input, quality, and sustainability guardrails.
5. Use written reviews and postmortems to improve the system rather than assign blame.
6. Revisit the organization when the constraint or product stage changes.

## Common Mistakes

- Hiring a growth team to manufacture product-market fit.
- Copying a large-company org chart without its scale or platforms.
- Making every employee a marketer without role clarity.
- Rewarding experiment volume instead of customer and business outcomes.
